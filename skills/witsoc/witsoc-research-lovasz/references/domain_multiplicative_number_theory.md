# Domain Playbook: Multiplicative Number Theory

Use for divisor sums, Euler phi, abundancy, perfect/multiperfect numbers, smoothness, prime factors, valuations, and multiplicative functions.

## First Normalizations

- factor `n = product p_i^a_i`,
- rewrite multiplicative functions as Euler products,
- isolate local prime-power factors,
- separate squarefree kernel from exponents,
- record `omega(n)`, `Omega(n)`, largest prime factor, smoothness, and valuation constraints.

## Theorem Families

- Gronwall/Robin-type divisor bounds,
- Mertens products,
- Zsigmondy primitive divisor theorem,
- LTE and valuation lemmas,
- CRT and local-global congruence,
- sieve bounds and parity barrier,
- smooth number estimates,
- results on perfect/multiperfect/abundant numbers.

## Common Barriers

- generic maximal-order bounds are sharp for arbitrary `n`,
- integrality constraints create dependency chains,
- primitive prime divisor exceptions,
- sieve parity barrier,
- local prime obstructions,
- exponent explosion,
- computation is sparse and misleading.

## First Experiments

- enumerate factorizations under smoothness bounds,
- compute multiplicative ratios exactly as rational numbers,
- build prime-dependency graphs from divisibility constraints,
- search for primitive divisor exceptions,
- test bounded exponent/squarefree-kernel cases.

## Product Ladder

1. local obstruction lemma,
2. bounded-exponent special case,
3. squarefree-kernel special case,
4. dependency-chain theorem,
5. conditional result under primitive-divisor assumptions,
6. computation certificate for a bounded search,
7. reduction from full problem to a graph/chain lemma.
