# Domain Playbook: Ramsey Theory

Use for forced monochromatic structures, coloring avoidance, Ramsey/Turán hybrids, van der Waerden-style questions, and extremal set-coloring.

## First Objects To Test

- random colorings,
- cyclic/coloring templates,
- blow-ups of small colorings,
- finite fields and affine geometries,
- greedy colorings,
- known lower-bound constructions.

## Theorem Families

- probabilistic method,
- Lovász local lemma,
- containers,
- dependent random choice,
- stepping-up/down lemmas,
- density Hales-Jewett and van der Waerden tools,
- hypergraph Ramsey bounds,
- finite field constructions.

## Common Barriers

- random lower bounds beat deterministic intuition,
- induction recurrences lose too much,
- container bounds lose constants/logs,
- diagonal and off-diagonal variants diverge,
- small exact cases do not predict asymptotics.

## First Experiments

- SAT encode colorings avoiding a target,
- random search near thresholds,
- minimize colorings/counterexamples,
- compute exact small cases,
- test blow-up stability.

## Product Ladder

1. exact small case,
2. SAT certificate for lower/upper finite bound,
3. counterexample to a stronger threshold,
4. conditional recurrence improvement,
5. construction family,
6. reduction to hypergraph/container statement.
