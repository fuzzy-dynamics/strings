# Domain Playbook: Additive Combinatorics

Use for sumsets, difference sets, arithmetic progressions, density increments, Fourier uniformity, finite abelian groups, and inverse problems.

## First Normalizations

- choose ambient group: integers, cyclic group, vector space over finite field;
- normalize density and scale;
- identify translation/dilation symmetries;
- separate structured and random components;
- test small groups before integer claims.

## Theorem Families

- Cauchy-Davenport/Kneser,
- Freiman/Ruzsa inverse theorems,
- Balog-Szemerédi-Gowers,
- Roth/Szemerédi density increment,
- Fourier uniformity and large spectrum,
- polynomial method,
- container/removal lemmas.

## Common Barriers

- density increment loses too much,
- inverse theorem too weak quantitatively,
- torsion variants differ from integer variants,
- Bohr set rank/radius losses,
- random sets show stronger variants false.

## First Experiments

- enumerate small cyclic groups,
- random dense/sparse subsets,
- compute sumset/difference-set sizes,
- test progression-free or configuration-free claims,
- minimize finite counterexamples.

## Product Ladder

1. finite group special case,
2. counterexample to stronger density claim,
3. weak quantitative bound,
4. inverse-structure lemma,
5. conditional theorem under uniformity assumption,
6. reduction to known inverse theorem.
